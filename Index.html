Interested in my autobiography?

I grew up in a small town in Antioch, California at the age of 3. I had high hopes of becoming a Dominoes delivery driver because I loved pizza so much. Eventually I knew that I had bigger ambitions that I wanted to pursue. In everything I did, I always had one value that stayed the same: "Work smarter, not harder".

I always saw myself wishing that I had more time to do things that I loved to do, this meant managing my time in a way where I can finish all my homework at school, so when I came home I could play sports and video games. That way whenever my Mom would ask "Are you done with your homework?" I could show her it and be on with my day. Now does that mean I was able to retain everything? Absolutely.

I graduated Deer Valley High School being the top 3% of my class. I was able to go to the University of California of Irvine under the Elgibility in the Local Context Program (ELC). Although I am still working towards my degree in Computer Science, I can say that I have gotten the most out of college experience and have no regrets. I would not have been able to survive my time at UC Irvine if I did not know how to manage my time efficiently. I worked in multiple jobs at campus, was a captain of my previous dance team, and network with multiple organizations and people to help me with the challenges that I will face in the real world.
